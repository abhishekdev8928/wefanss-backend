The Story of Bhishma Pitamah: A Life of Sacrifice and Duty— Understanding Life Choices with Career Astrology Consultation

Bhishma Pitamah was originally known by the name Devavrata. His parents were King Shantanu of Hastinapura and the river goddess Ganga. The Hindu texts mention that he was an extraordinary warrior, scholar, and statesman.

However, his life took a pivotal turn when he took the vow of celibacy to ensure his father's marriage to Satyavati. He renounced his claim to the throne of Hastinapura.

This sacrifice earned him the name Bhishma. The word means “the one who undertakes a severe vow.” His father blessed him with the boon of choosing the moment of his death (Ichcha Mrityu).

During the Mahabharata war, Bhishma fought valiantly on the side of the Kauravas (despite his personal affection for the Pandavas). He was invincible in battle and resisted the Pandavas' advances for ten days.

Ultimately, he was felled by Arjuna, assisted by Shikhandi, during the war. Bhishma lay on a bed of arrows for over fifty days. During that time, he offered counsel to the Pandavas on Shanti Parva and Anushasana Parva. He imparted invaluable wisdom on dharma, governance, and the nature of life and death to them.

As the day of his death approached, Bhishma chose the auspicious ‘Magha Shukla Ashtami’ for his departure. Uttarayan is the period when the Sun begins its northward journey. It is a time believed to be spiritually favourable for attaining moksha. This decision, marking his final act of supreme devotion and sacrifice, is the essence of “Bhishma Ashtami.”

Rituals and Observances on “Bhishma Ashtami”

On “Bhishma Ashtami”, devotees perform several holy rituals to pay reverence to Bhishma Pitamah. Performing fasting and offering prayers on this day offers many spiritual benefits. People also perform a special rite known as ‘Tarpan’ as well. It is a ritual to appease one's ancestors.

Let's learn more about these rituals in detail below.

1. Ekodishta Shradh (Shradh for Bhishma)

‘Ekodishta Shradh’ is one of the most important rituals of “Bhishma Ashtami” to honour Bhishma Pitamah. If you have lost your father, performing this rite is very important for you.

***Important: While Vedic texts mention that ‘Ekodishta Shradh’ is to be performed by people with deceased fathers, many communities choose to perform it regardless of their father’s status. They believe that it brings blessings for the entire family.

2. Tarpan and Offering Prayers at River Banks

‘Tarpan’ is also a significant part of “Bhishma Ashtami” rituals. To perform this, the devotees offer water, sesame seeds, and other sacred items in a river. Typically, people choose to perform it in the Ganga River (Mother- Mata of Bhishma Pitamah).

‘Tarpan’ honours Bhishma Pitamah and one's ancestors. It brings peace to the souls of the departed and purifies the performer. People make the offering with the hope that the blessings of Bhishma Pitamah will lead to spiritual liberation and remove any obstacles from their lives.

3. Fasting and Ritual Observances

Many devotees also observe a fast on “Bhishma Ashtami.” They abstain from food and water for the day. The holy texts mention that the fast purifies the body and mind. It also fosters a connection to Bhishma’s spirit of sacrifice and devotion.

During the fast, people offer Arghyam. It is a sacred offering of water. They also chant the Bhishma Ashtami mantra "Evasunamavataraya Shantoratmajay Ch.” It imparts Bhishma’s blessings for righteousness and wisdom.

4. Taking a Holy Dip in the Ganges

When people come to the shores of the Ganga River to perform Tarpan on “Bhishma Ashtami”, they also take a dip in the sacred waters. This observance has purifying powers. It washes away all the sins and provides spiritual merits. By doing so, devotees also align themselves with the purity and sanctity that Bhishma himself embodied throughout his life.
